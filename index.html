Our Heritage & Story

Connect. Empower. Transform.

Established on 3rd July 1939 under the reign of the Travancore King, Sri Chithira Thirunal Balarama Varma, the College of Engineering Trivandrum (CET) was the first engineering college in the former Travancore State. Initially housed in the Chief Engineer's bungalow (present PMG office) under its first Principal, Maj T.H. Mathewman, it began with 21 degree students. In 1960, CET shifted to its present sprawling 125-acre campus.

Today, under the APJ Abdul Kalam Technological University, CET has grown to around 4,500 students, 311 faculty, and 290 staff. Offered degrees include B.Tech, B.Arch, M.Tech, M.Plan, MCA, and MBA. Ranking 71st in the NIRF National Rankings, CET remains a leading technical institution and the most sought-after destination for engineering education in Kerala.

President's Message

Dr. Suresh K., Principal of CET & President of CETAA

College of Engineering Trivandrum, established in the year 1939 has completed 81 glorious years. The institution has been imparting quality engineering education to students over these years resulting in more than 48,000 alumni shining in various professions across the globe. Our alumni include eminent engineers, great scientists, renowned academicians, top bureaucrats, industrialists, and entrepreneurs.

College of Engineering Trivandrum had a humble beginning with three Engineering branches: namely Civil, Mechanical and Electrical Engineering with 18 students in each branch.

Over these years, CET has been elevated to one of the top Engineering institutions of India. Currently CET offers 7 B. Tech programs, 27 different M. Tech specialisations, B. Arch, M. Arch, MCA, MBA and doctoral programs comprising of 4500 students, 300 faculty members and 250 nonteaching staff.

For further enhancement of institutional success, we are moving forward with our vision of 'Institution of excellence with International repute' having National level excellence and International Visibility in every facet of Engineering Research and Education.

It is really encouraging that, in the National Institutional Ranking Framework called NIRF, established by Government of India, which measures the National level excellence of Academic Institutions and Universities, we were ranked 85th in the Engineering category and 11th in the architecture category at the National level. We could achieve this only because of our excellent teaching learning resources, better academic performance of our students and their campus placements.

It must be noted that IIT Chennai, the one which topped in the NIRF ranking has an annual budget of nearly Rupees 700 crores versus our annual budget is around 100 crores, and fully funded by the Government of Kerala. IIT Chennai has more than 2000 Ph.D. students but we have only 200 regular Ph.D. students in the campus. 40% weightage in the ranking process is for research related activities and the resulting outcomes.

In the coming years, we aim to achieve a higher position, to be in the top 30 at the national level. CET has to be transformed into a top tier research and learning institution with state-of-the-art facilities and industry linkage. In this regard, our Alumni can play a major role.

The greatest treasure that an institution cherishes and relies upon is its alumni. College of Engineering Trivandrum Alumni Association (CETAA) was initiated in the year 1976. They ensure support to deserving students through the Growing Engineers Monetary Support (GEMS) scheme. In the past 10 years, CETAA has provided GEMS scholarship to 300 students, each student being given Rs 10,000/- per year.

I would like to appreciate the great social initiative, the CETAA HOPE project led by the UAE chapter who support several school, college and professional students financially, and the prompt COVID-19 pandemic response where our alumni donated 250 laptops for deserving students, raising nearly 75 lakhs within a month.

Apart from financial support, our alumni now are directly involved in conducting various programmes to enhance the skills of our students. As the Principal of CET, I am proud of you, my dear friends. Your continued support with your time, talents and funds is most appreciated.

Dr. SURESH K

Principal, College of Engineering Trivandrum
