Renovation Proposal · 2025

Diamond Jubilee Hall

College of Engineering Trivandrum — Proposed renovation including air-conditioning, acoustics upgrade, and ancillary facilities

Project Ref: CET/INFRA/DJH/2025
Capacity: 500 seats
Area: ~1,800 sq.m
Stage: Pre-DPR
Estimated cost ₹4.2 Cr Civil + MEP + FF&E
Duration 14 months 4 phased packages
Cooling load 120 TR VRF + AHU hybrid
New ancillary area 640 sq.m Green rooms, foyer, store
Existing condition

Current limitations

  • Ceiling fans leading to high ambient noise and poor thermal comfort.
  • No acoustic wall or ceiling treatments causing echo and reverberation.
  • Lack of dedicated green rooms for performers.
  • No accessible sanitation facilities for differently-abled guests.
Post-renovation

Design objectives

  • ASHRAE 55 thermal comfort compliance via central air-conditioning.
  • RT60 acoustics targets optimized for both speech and musical events.
  • New ancillary block with modern green rooms and utility support.
  • Inclusive design featuring accessible sanitation and retractable wheelchair seating.
Structural

Civil works

  • Comprehensive roof repair and structural waterproofing.
  • Installing insulated false ceiling structure to support HVAC.
  • Acoustic louvre sealing to isolate exterior environmental noise.
  • Floor levelling and building a glazed linkage corridor.
  • Extended main lobby area for reception.
Electrical

Power & lighting

  • Dedicated 100 kVA distribution transformer on site.
  • Professional LED theatrical stage and ambient house lighting.
  • Heavy-duty 32A utility sockets for high-power AV equipment.
  • Grid-tied 60 kW solar PV system for sustainable operations.
Safety

Fire & life safety

  • Addressable fire alarm system with thermal and smoke detectors.
  • Automatic wet sprinkler network throughout the hall and lobby.
  • Upgraded emergency exits fitted with single-press panic bars.

System Selection Rationale

The Diamond Jubilee Hall features intermittent occupancy profiles with high peak loads during large events and low loads during smaller meetings. A VRF (Variable Refrigerant Flow) and central AHU (Air Handling Unit) hybrid configuration was selected to optimize part-load energy efficiency, accommodate quick pre-cooling schedules, and ensure silent, vibration-free acoustic operations inside the main auditorium.

Technical HVAC Specifications

System type VRF + central AHU
Total cooling capacity 120 TR (421 kW)
Main hall AHU capacity 80 TR, 28,000 CFM
Ancillary FCU capacity 40 TR across 8 FCUs
Refrigerant R-410A / R-32
Design indoor temp 24°C ± 1, 55% RH
Outdoor design temp 36°C DB / 28°C WB
Fresh air provision 8 L/s per person (ASHRAE 62.1)
Energy efficiency (EER) ≥ 3.5 (5-star BEE)
Supply Air
Supply air distribution

Linear slot diffusers integrated along false ceiling edges, high-induction return air grilles, and low-velocity under-seat ducting for uniform cooling.

Stage HVAC
Stage HVAC

Dedicated separate FCU circuit to prevent draft on performers, and low-noise swirl diffusers integrated into the stage floor plenum.

Controls
Controls

BACnet BMS integration, occupancy sensors, CO₂ demand-controlled ventilation (DCV), and automated pre-conditioning scheduling.

Ancillary Wing Master Plan

Constructing a new 640 sq.m single-storey ancillary block on the north side, structurally independent but seamlessly connected to the main hall via a glazed link corridor. Designed in strict compliance with Kerala PWD norms and IS 11600 universal accessibility guidelines.

Backstage

Green rooms ×2

Dedicated makeup and dressing spaces for artists and VIP speakers.

  • 40 sq.m each with en-suite toilets.
  • Theatrical mirror lighting and charging ports.
  • Silent 1.5 TR split AC units.
  • Direct secure access to stage wings.
Technical

AV control room

The command center for live audio, projection, and recording feeds.

  • 25 sq.m soundproof enclosure.
  • Double-glazed wide view of the stage.
  • Raised access flooring for cable runs.
  • 30 kVA online UPS for AV backup.
  • Rack space for consoles and amplifiers.
Storage

Equipment store

Secure staging area for stage props, furniture, and AV gear.

  • 60 sq.m storage space.
  • Double-leaf 2.4 m wide entry doors.
  • Ceiling hoist with 500 kg lifting capacity.
  • Anti-static heavy-duty epoxy floor.
Public

Covered foyer

Welcoming lobby space for registration and event networking.

  • 120 sq.m of high-ceiling lobby area.
  • Local laterite stone feature cladding.
  • Integrated ticket and registration counter.
  • Premium guest seating lounge layout.
Sanitation

Toilet block

Modernized, hygienic sanitary utilities for maximum capacity loads.

  • 4 WCs (Male) + 4 WCs (Female).
  • 4 low-flow sensor urinals.
  • 4+4 ceramic wash basins.
  • One fully compliant accessible unisex toilet with grab bars.
Utilities

Plantroom

Supporting services infrastructure to feed utility demands.

  • 30 sq.m building plant room.
  • VRF outdoor unit structural foundations.
  • Electrical main distribution board (MDB).
  • Acoustically isolated 125 kVA generator enclosure (noise levels ≤50 dB(A)).
Seating

Upholstered auditorium chairs

Ergonomic, heavy-duty seating matching the campus colors:

  • High-density padded tip-up mechanism.
  • Class 1 fire-rated acoustic fabric upholstery.
  • Comfortable 900 mm row-to-row spacing.
  • 480 fixed seats + 20 retractable wheelchair-friendly bays.
  • Integrated USB-A charging ports.
Walls

Acoustic wall treatment

Premium audio isolation and spatial diffusion design:

  • 50 mm glass-wool backing with mineral-wool absorbers (NRC ≥ 0.90).
  • Perforated timber sound diffusion panels.
  • Impact-resistant local laterite dados up to 1.2 m height.
Ceiling

Coffered false ceiling

Structural ceiling redesign optimized for lighting and acoustics:

  • Galvanized iron (GI) framed gypsum acoustic ceiling boards.
  • 5.5 m vertical stage clearance.
  • Integrated continuous HVAC linear slots.
  • 50 mm glass-wool backing for enhanced absorption.
  • Ceiling planes angled at 3° towards the stage for sound projection.
Floor

Raked floor

Optimized sightlines and structural layout:

  • Gradual 1:12 slope raking starting from row 5.
  • Lightweight raised-access structural flooring.
  • Heavy-duty acoustic carpet tile finish (NRC 0.35).
  • High-contrast anti-slip vinyl walkways and LED aisle markers.
Stage

Stage & proscenium

Stage craft structures and finishes:

  • Glass Reinforced Concrete (GRC) proscenium arch with integrated LED tracks.
  • 18 mm teak-ply spring-isolated floating stage floor.
  • Velvet main draw curtain and acoustic cyclorama.
  • Provision for modular orchestra pit covers.
AV

Sound & projection

High-definition multimedia integration:

  • Professional line-array speakers (L/R mains + front fills + subwoofers).
  • 4K high-brightness laser projector.
  • 10 m tensioned motorized screen.
  • 4-camera HDMI live stream routing matrix.
  • Assistive hearing loop (IEC 60118-4) for inclusivity.
Target Acoustic Performance Summary
RT60 speech: 0.9–1.1 s
RT60 music: 1.2–1.4 s
Background noise: NC-30
STI: ≥ 0.65

Phasing Strategy

The 14-month construction schedule is strictly aligned with the College of Engineering Trivandrum academic calendar. Heavy civil operations, demolition works, and major utility shutdowns are scheduled during vacation periods to prevent campus disruptions, ensuring the safety of students and staff.

Phase 4 (Months 12-14)
Phase 1

Demolition & structure

Months 1–3
  • Removal of old ceiling fans, wall paneling, and existing chairs.
  • Roof structural waterproofing and crack repairs.
  • Excavation and foundation works for the northern ancillary block.
  • Erection of temporary safety hoardings.
Phase 2

Civil & ancillary construction

Months 4–7
  • Superstructure building works for the new 640 sq.m ancillary wing.
  • False ceiling GI framework installation.
  • Floor raking concrete pours starting from row 5.
  • GRC proscenium arch casting and lobby linkages.
  • Electrical conduit and HVAC rough-ins.
Phase 3

MEP fit-out & systems installation

Months 8–11
  • HVAC AHU placing, VRF piping, and ductwork insulation.
  • Electrical wiring, switchgear, and main distribution boards setup.
  • Fire alarm and sprinkler piping installation.
  • AV wiring, line-array speakers, and projection screen fitting.
  • Acoustic wall paneling install and initial RT60 validation testing.
Phase 4

FF&E, finishing & handover

Months 12–14
  • Auditorium seating installation (480 fixed + 20 wheelchair spaces).
  • Green room final fit-outs, lighting, and mirror settings.
  • Foyer paving, ticket counter setup, and landscaping.
  • Snagging checklist resolution and cleaning.
  • Integrated systems test (HVAC + Acoustic + AV + Fire Safety).
  • Final commissioning, handover, and inaugural ceremony preparation.
